# Page 4 — Contact Us

**Goal of the page.** Tell the reader exactly how to reach us,
and give them confidence that an email will get a real reply.
This is the last page, so it has the most white space — the eye
should land on the email address.

---

## Content

### Page title

> Contact Us

### Centerpiece — what to type into the empty center of the page

The blank space in the middle of the page is the most important
block on the flyer. It holds **one short paragraph**, **one
button-style email address**, and **three lines** of contact
detail. Everything else on this page supports those three blocks.

**Center paragraph (above the email)**

> Tell us about your project. We will reply within two business
> days with a written proposal — scope, timeline, and price.

> `[CONFIRM]` *Two business days is the suggested SLA. The business*
> *owner needs to confirm before this ships.*

**Big email block (the visual anchor)**

> contact@dodao.io

> `[CONFIRM]` *Replace with the real address that actually receives*
> *mail. Use a monitored alias, not a personal inbox.*

**Three lines under the email**

> Website:  https://dodao.io
> Book a call:  https://calendly.com/dodao  ← `[CONFIRM]` real link
> LinkedIn:  https://linkedin.com/company/dodao  ← `[CONFIRM]` real handle

### Section — How working with us starts

A three-step numbered list. Each step is one sentence so the whole
process fits in five lines.

**Section heading**

> How Working With Us Starts

**Step 1 — You send a problem statement**

> Email us a one-paragraph description of what you are building
> and what you need help with. Attach a diagram or photo if you
> have one.

**Step 2 — We send a written proposal**

> Within two business days we reply with scope, timeline, price,
> and what we will need from you to start.

**Step 3 — You pick an option and we begin**

> You pick the option that fits your budget. We sign a short
> agreement and work begins the following week.

### What to send us (a small checklist)

A short list, italicised, sitting under the three-step flow.

> *Useful to include in the first email: the robot you are using
> (or considering), the task in one sentence, the deadline, and
> whether you need simulation setup, synthetic data, or both.*

### Footer

> For more information, visit https://dodao.io     Page 4 of 4

**Block-by-block placement**

| # | Block | Position | Notes |
|---|-------|----------|-------|
| 1 | DoDAO logo | Top-right | Same as the other pages. |
| 2 | Page title "Contact Us" | Top-left, 36 pt | Accent bar on the left. |
| 3 | Center paragraph | **Vertically centered** in the upper half | Center-aligned text, ~480 px max width. |
| 4 | Big email block | **Directly below** the paragraph, center-aligned | The most visually prominent element on the whole flyer. Treat as a pill button: 18–22 pt, plenty of internal padding, optional border in `#5F86FF`. |
| 5 | Three contact lines | **Below** the email block, center-aligned | 11 pt, three lines, generous line height. |
| 6 | "How Working With Us Starts" | Lower half of the page | Left-aligned again. 3 numbered bullets. |
| 7 | Checklist line | Below the 3 steps | Italic, 10 pt. One line. |
| 8 | Footer | Bottom | URL left, page number right. |

**Center alignment notes.** Blocks 3, 4, and 5 are the only
center-aligned content in the whole flyer. That asymmetry is on
purpose — it makes the email address pop. The rest of the page
keeps the left-aligned grid of Pages 1–3 so the set still feels
like one document.

**The empty space matters.** Do not fill the white space around
the email block with decorative elements. The blank space is what
makes the email block the eye-magnet.

**Before exporting:** replace every line tagged `[CONFIRM]` with
the real address, link, or value. Do not ship the flyer with
placeholder text.
